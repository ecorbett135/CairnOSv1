# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

CairnOSv1 is built around careful trail judgment, transparent data, and
respectful collaboration. We expect contributors, testers, and maintainers to
help keep the project welcoming, practical, and focused on improving the tool.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and constructive.
- Assume good intent, especially when discussing trail experience, data quality,
  safety concerns, or planning preferences.
- Give feedback that is specific enough to act on.
- Respect different hiking styles, physical abilities, risk tolerances, and
  levels of technical experience.
- Keep safety-critical discussions grounded in official sources, verifiable
  field observations, or clearly labeled personal experience.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, insults, threats, or discriminatory language.
- Dismissing safety concerns without evidence.
- Sharing private information without permission.
- Repeated off-topic promotion or spam.
- Treating alpha planner output as authoritative field guidance.

## Reporting

If you see behavior that violates this code of conduct, contact the maintainer
privately rather than escalating in public threads when possible. Include links,
screenshots, or context if available.

Maintainers may remove comments, close issues, or restrict participation to keep
the project healthy and focused.

## Attribution

This code of conduct is inspired by the Contributor Covenant, simplified for an
early alpha open-source project.
